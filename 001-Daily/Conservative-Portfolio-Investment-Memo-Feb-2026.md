- GDP growth target: ~5% for 2026
- Fiscal stimulus: Record-level central government transfers
- Property market stabilization: Mortgage subsidies, tax rebates
- Export resilience: External demand sustained for third consecutive year

### Bear Case (Cross-Referenced Reality)

**Property Sector Implosion:** Despite policy support, domestic confidence indicators remain weak. Property downturn creates negative wealth effects, flatlining rental growth, and entrenched deflationary expectations.

**Deflationary Feedback Loop:** Overcapacity → aggressive price wars → weak profit growth → cost-cutting/layoffs → weaker household confidence → higher savings → softer domestic demand → worsening overcapacity.

**Fiscal Transmission Failure:** Despite record fiscal deficit expansion, transmission into real economic activity has been weaker than anticipated. Local government capacity declined under debt relief pressures.

**Export Headwinds:** Expanding trade surplus fuels protectionism backlash. Voluntary export restraints (Japan 1980s playbook) likely, capping growth engine.

**Bond Market Signals:** China 10Y yield at 1.79% reflects excessive pessimism, strengthening deflation expectations, and weak borrowing demand.

**Yuan Depreciation Risk:** Bearish positioning at April 2026 highs. Capital outflow pressures could widen USD/CNY yield spreads.

### Consensus: L-Shaped Recovery Most Likely

V- or U-shaped recovery appears improbable. An L-shaped trough establishment is the realistic bull case for 2026.
